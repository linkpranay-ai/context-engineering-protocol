# CMS-100: Synthetic Demo Configuration Management Process Standard

This is a fictional, hand-authored process standard used only to dogfood the How-L1 mechanism. It
imitates the clause-numbering conventions of documents like CMMI appraisal materials or an ISO 9001
quality manual — it is not derived from, or a substitute for, any real standard.

## 5 Configuration Management

Configuration management establishes and maintains the integrity of a project's work products
throughout their lifecycle, from initial baseline through every subsequent revision.

### 5.1 Baseline Identification

A baseline shall be established for each work product upon completion of its defining review.
Every baseline is uniquely identified and, once established, is placed under configuration
control — no further changes may be made to a baselined work product except through the change
control procedure defined below.

### 5.2 Change Control

All changes to a baselined work product shall be evaluated, approved, and tracked before being
incorporated (see 5.1). A change control board reviews each proposed change against the
baseline's original acceptance criteria and records the disposition — approved, rejected, or
deferred — before any change is merged.

### 5.3 Status Accounting

The current status and history of every baseline shall be recorded and made available to
affected stakeholders, including a traceable record of every approved change and its rationale.

## 6 Verification

### 6.1 Peer Review

Every work product shall undergo peer review by at least one qualified reviewer other than its
author prior to being proposed as a baseline candidate. Review findings are recorded and tracked
to closure.
